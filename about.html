Our Story

We believe every voice
deserves to be heard

Fluent Minds was founded to dismantle the shame around stuttering and replace it with tools, community, and confidence — especially for students in Bangladesh.

"The world needs your ideas — don't let a stutter silence them."

Born from lived experience

Fluent Minds was founded by a student who stuttered through school — avoiding oral answers, dreading presentations, and whispering when they wanted to shout. The experience was isolating. But it didn't have to be.

After discovering that stuttering is a neurological difference — not a flaw, not a weakness, not something to be "fixed" through shame — the founding team set out to create the resource they wished had existed: a welcoming, evidence-based, honest space for young people who stutter.

Today, Fluent Minds publishes articles, guides, and stories that help students across Bangladesh and South Asia find their voice — not by eliminating their stutter, but by refusing to let it define the limits of what they can say and do.

What We Stand For

Our Values

🔬

Evidence First

Every article draws from peer-reviewed speech pathology research. We never promote unproven cures or false hope — only what actually helps.

🤝

Community Over Isolation

Stuttering thrives in shame and silence. We build spaces where students feel seen, understood, and supported — not judged.

🌱

Confidence, Not Cure

We don't promise fluency. We teach students to pursue expression — to say what they mean, even if it takes longer to say it.

📖

Accessible Knowledge

Speech therapy is expensive and inaccessible for many in Bangladesh. We make high-quality guidance free for everyone, always.

💬

Honest Storytelling

We share real struggles alongside real wins. No toxic positivity — just honest stories from people who understand what it's like.

✊

Student-Led

Our content is shaped by students who stutter, not observers. Lived experience is at the heart of everything we publish.

Founder & Editor

Kazi Nadid Hossain

Computer Science Student · University of Dhaka

Nadid founded Fluent Minds after years of navigating school, olympiads, and public life as someone who stutters. A medalist at IChO 2024, EuPhO 2023, and competitive programming champion, Nadid knows firsthand that a stutter has nothing to do with intelligence, capability, or potential. Fluent Minds is his effort to make sure every high school student in Bangladesh knows the same.
